Against Worry

Marcus Aurelius advises against worrying about the future. The Stoic philosophy suggests that a wise and virtuous person lives in accordance with nature and does not fear external events such as pain, death, or poverty. He emphasizes the importance of focusing on moral responsibility and being a complete human being.


Since the the future is beyond one's control worrying about it is counterproductive and futile. Stoicism encourages individuals to concentrate on what they can control, such as their reactions to events, rather than expending energy on anxieties about uncertain future outcomes. The emphasis is on accepting the inevitability of certain events, including adversity and death, and facing them with courage and virtue when they occur.
